In 'The Shining', a struggling writer named Jack Torrance accepts a job as the
winter caretaker of the secluded Overlook Hotel with his family. As supernatural
forces gradually possess Jack, his son Danny's psychic abilities reveal the
hotel's dark past, leading to a chilling descent into madness and terror.

## Why we liked it 👍
- Masterful building of tension and atmosphere
- Complex characters that delve into psychological horror

## Why it might not work for you 👎
- Slow pacing may not appeal to all readers
- Some graphic and disturbing scenes may be unsettling for some

## Full spoiler-free review
Stephen King's 'The Shining' is a chilling masterpiece of horror literature that
delves deep into the psychological unraveling of its characters. King's skillful
prose weaves a tale of isolation, madness, and supernatural forces, creating an
atmosphere of dread that lingers long after the final page. The Overlook Hotel
itself becomes a character, its haunted history seeping into every corner of the
narrative. Jack Torrance's descent into madness is both harrowing and
compelling, while Danny's psychic abilities add an extra layer of intrigue to
the story. While the slow pacing may deter some readers, those who appreciate a
slow burn and intricate character development will find 'The Shining' to be a
haunting and unforgettable read.
