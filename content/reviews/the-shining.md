In 'The Shining,' a struggling writer named Jack Torrance takes a job as the
winter caretaker of the Overlook Hotel, where he, his wife Wendy, and their
young son Danny soon discover the hotel's dark and malevolent secrets. As the
isolation and supernatural forces within the hotel begin to take their toll,
Jack's sanity unravels, leading to a terrifying climax of psychological horror
and violence.

## Why we liked it 👍
- Masterful atmosphere building that creates an intense sense of dread.
- Complex characters with inner struggles and dark pasts.

## Why it might not work for you 👎
- Some may find the pacing slow, especially in the beginning.
- Certain supernatural elements may be unsettling for sensitive readers.

## Full spoiler-free review
Stephen King's 'The Shining' is a chilling masterpiece that delves deep into the
psyche of its characters, painting a vivid portrait of their descent into
madness within the eerie confines of the Overlook Hotel. King's skillful prose
and meticulous attention to detail create a palpable sense of dread that lingers
long after the final page. The characters, particularly Jack Torrance, are
expertly fleshed out, making their psychological unraveling all the more
disturbing to witness. While the pacing may feel sluggish at times, the slow
burn only serves to heighten the tension as the horror gradually escalates to a
heart-pounding climax. 'The Shining' is a must-read for fans of psychological
horror, offering a haunting and unforgettable journey into the darkest recesses
of the human mind.
