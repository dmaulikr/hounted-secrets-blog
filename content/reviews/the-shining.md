In 'The Shining,' Stephen King tells the chilling tale of the Torrance family
who become caretakers of the remote Overlook Hotel during the winter. As the
supernatural forces within the hotel begin to manifest, the family's sanity is
tested, leading to a terrifying climax of psychological horror and suspense.

## Why we liked it 👍
- Masterful build-up of tension and suspense
- Complex characters that add depth to the story

## Why it might not work for you 👎
- Some may find the pacing slow in parts
- The supernatural elements may be too intense for some readers

## Full spoiler-free review
Stephen King's 'The Shining' is a winter-cabin masterpiece that delves deep into
the psychological horrors of the human mind. King's masterful storytelling keeps
readers on edge as the tension builds slowly, creating an atmosphere of dread
that permeates every page. The characters, especially the troubled Jack
Torrance, are complex and compelling, adding layers of depth to the narrative.
However, some readers may find the pacing a bit slow in parts, as King takes his
time to unravel the mysteries of the haunted Overlook Hotel. The supernatural
elements are intense and may be unsettling for some, but they add an extra layer
of fear to the story. Overall, 'The Shining' is a classic horror novel that will
haunt readers long after they've turned the final page.
