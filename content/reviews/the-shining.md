In 'The Shining,' Jack Torrance takes on the job of caretaker at the isolated
Overlook Hotel during the winter months. As the hotel's dark history begins to
unravel, Jack's sanity is tested while his son, Danny, discovers his psychic
abilities. The family's descent into madness is a chilling exploration of the
human psyche and the supernatural forces at play within the hotel's walls.

## Why we liked it 👍

## Why it might not work for you 👎

## Full spoiler-free review
Stephen King's 'The Shining' is a haunting tale that delves into the
psychological horrors of isolation, addiction, and the sinister forces lurking
within the Overlook Hotel. The atmospheric setting and intricate character
development draw readers into a world where reality blurs with the supernatural,
creating a sense of unease that lingers long after the final page. King's
evocative prose and expert pacing build tension steadily, leading to a chilling
climax that will leave readers on edge. While the slow pacing may test some
readers' patience, the payoff is worth the journey. 'The Shining' is a classic
horror novel that continues to terrify and captivate readers with its timeless
tale of terror and madness.
