In 'The Shining,' Jack Torrance takes a job as the winter caretaker of the
Overlook Hotel, bringing his family along with him. As the hotel's dark history
begins to reveal itself, Jack's sanity deteriorates, putting his wife Wendy and
son Danny in grave danger.

## Why we liked it 👍
- Masterful atmosphere and tension-building that keeps readers on edge
- Complex characters that add depth to the chilling narrative

## Why it might not work for you 👎
- Some readers may find the pacing slow in certain parts
- The supernatural elements may be too intense for sensitive readers

## Full spoiler-free review
Stephen King's 'The Shining' is a haunting masterpiece that delves deep into the
psyche of its characters, particularly Jack Torrance. The isolation of the
Overlook Hotel, coupled with the supernatural forces at play, creates a sense of
dread that permeates every page. King's writing is atmospheric and immersive,
drawing readers into a world where reality blurs with madness. The slow
unraveling of Jack's descent into madness is both fascinating and terrifying,
making 'The Shining' a psychological horror classic. While some may find the
pacing a bit slow, the payoff is worth it for those who appreciate a slow burn
horror story. Overall, 'The Shining' is a gripping tale of terror that will
linger in your thoughts long after you've finished reading.
