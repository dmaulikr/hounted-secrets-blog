Jack Torrance, a struggling writer and recovering alcoholic, takes a winter
caretaker job at the isolated Overlook Hotel with his family. As the hotel's
dark history begins to influence Jack, his son Danny's psychic abilities reveal
terrifying secrets within the hotel's walls.

## Why we liked it 👍
- Intense and atmospheric setting that slowly builds tension.
- Complex character development that delves into the psychological horror of the human mind.

## Why it might not work for you 👎
- Some may find the pacing slow in the beginning.
- Certain supernatural elements may be unsettling for sensitive readers.

## Full spoiler-free review
Stephen King's 'The Shining' is a chilling tale of isolation, madness, and the
supernatural. The Overlook Hotel serves as a sinister backdrop for the
unraveling of the Torrance family as they confront their inner demons and the
malevolent forces at play. King's masterful storytelling keeps readers on edge,
with a slow burn of tension that culminates in a terrifying climax. The
characters are deeply flawed and compelling, adding layers of complexity to the
narrative. While the pacing may be considered slow by some, the gradual descent
into madness is a crucial element of the story's mounting dread. 'The Shining'
is a haunting exploration of the darkness that lurks within us all, making it a
must-read for fans of psychological horror.
