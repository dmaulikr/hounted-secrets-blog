In 'The Shining,' author Stephen King tells the chilling tale of the Torrance
family's stay at the isolated Overlook Hotel during the winter. As the
patriarch, Jack, grapples with his inner demons, the supernatural forces within
the hotel begin to unravel the family's sanity, leading to a terrifying climax.

## Full spoiler-free review
Stephen King's 'The Shining' is a winter-cabin masterpiece that delves deep into
the realms of psychological horror. King's atmospheric writing creates a
palpable sense of dread that lingers long after the final page. The characters,
especially Jack Torrance, are intricately developed, making their descent into
madness all the more chilling. While the pacing may seem slow at times, it
ultimately serves to heighten the suspense and build towards the heart-pounding
climax. However, sensitive readers should be aware that some scenes are
incredibly intense and may linger in the mind long after finishing the book.
Overall, 'The Shining' is a must-read for horror aficionados looking for a
gripping and psychologically unsettling tale.
