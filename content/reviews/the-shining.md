In 'The Shining,' Jack Torrance takes a caretaker job at the remote Overlook
Hotel during the off-season, where his family soon discovers the dark secrets
lurking within its walls. As the hotel's supernatural forces begin to manipulate
Jack's sanity, his young son Danny's psychic abilities become crucial for their
survival against the malevolent spirits haunting the establishment.

## Full spoiler-free review
Stephen King's 'The Shining' is a chilling psychological thriller that delves
deep into the horrors of isolation, madness, and the darkness of the human
psyche. The eerie setting of the Overlook Hotel serves as a perfect backdrop for
the escalating sense of dread that permeates the narrative. King's intricate
character development creates a haunting atmosphere that keeps readers on edge,
empathizing with the characters' descent into terror. While the pacing may drag
at times, the slow burn only adds to the tension, leading to a climactic and
unforgettable conclusion. 'The Shining' is a haunting masterpiece of horror
fiction that will linger in your thoughts long after you turn the final page.
