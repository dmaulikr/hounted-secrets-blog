In 'The Shining,' Jack Torrance takes a job as the winter caretaker of the
Overlook Hotel, where he, his wife Wendy, and their son Danny are isolated. As
the hotel's malevolent forces begin to possess Jack, Danny's psychic abilities
reveal the dark secrets of the hotel and its violent past.

## Why we liked it 👍
- Masterful atmosphere and tension building.
- Complex characters and psychological depth.

## Why it might not work for you 👎
- Some readers may find the pacing slow.
- Certain scenes may be too intense for sensitive readers.

## Full spoiler-free review
Stephen King's 'The Shining' is a chilling masterpiece that delves deep into the
twisted psyche of its characters. The atmospheric setting of the snowbound
Overlook Hotel serves as a perfect backdrop for the escalating horror that
unfolds within its walls. King's skillful storytelling keeps readers on edge, as
the tension gradually builds towards a terrifying climax. The characters,
particularly Jack Torrance and his son Danny, are intricately developed, adding
layers of psychological depth to the narrative. While the pacing may be slow for
some, the payoff is well worth it as the story unfolds with increasing dread.
'The Shining' is a gripping and haunting tale that will linger in the minds of
readers long after they turn the final page.
