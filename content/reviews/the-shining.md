In 'The Shining,' Jack Torrance takes a job as the winter caretaker of the
Overlook Hotel, where he, his wife Wendy, and their young son Danny are
isolated. As the hotel's dark history begins to influence Jack, Danny's psychic
abilities reveal terrifying secrets. The family must confront their own demons
and the malevolent forces within the hotel to survive.

## Full spoiler-free review
Stephen King's 'The Shining' is a chilling masterpiece of psychological horror.
The isolation of the Overlook Hotel serves as the perfect setting for the slow
unraveling of the Torrance family's sanity. King expertly weaves a narrative
that delves into themes of addiction, family dynamics, and the darkness of the
human psyche. The characters are richly developed, with Jack Torrance's descent
into madness being both terrifying and tragic. The supernatural elements are
seamlessly integrated, adding an extra layer of dread to the story. While the
pacing may be considered slow by some, the tension steadily mounts, leading to a
gripping and unforgettable climax. 'The Shining' is a must-read for horror fans,
showcasing King's mastery of the genre.
