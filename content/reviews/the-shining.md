In 'The Shining,' a struggling writer named Jack Torrance takes a job as the
winter caretaker of the Overlook Hotel, along with his wife Wendy and son Danny.
As the hotel's sinister history begins to unravel, Jack's sanity deteriorates,
leading to a terrifying climax that explores themes of isolation, addiction, and
the darkness of the human psyche.

## Why we liked it 👍
- Intense and atmospheric storytelling that keeps readers on edge.

## Full spoiler-free review
Stephen King's 'The Shining' is a chilling masterpiece of psychological horror
that delves deep into the unraveling mind of its protagonist. King's skillful
writing creates a palpable sense of dread that lingers long after the final
page. The characters are fleshed out with intricate detail, making their descent
into madness all the more compelling. The Overlook Hotel itself becomes a
character, its malevolent presence seeping into every corner of the narrative.
While the pacing may drag at times, the slow build only serves to heighten the
tension until the explosive and terrifying finale. 'The Shining' is a must-read
for fans of horror, offering a haunting exploration of the human psyche and the
darkness that resides within us all.
