The Shining follows Jack Torrance, a struggling writer who takes a job as the
winter caretaker of the Overlook Hotel with his family. As they become isolated
in the snow-covered hotel, supernatural forces begin to torment them, unraveling
Jack's sanity and putting his wife and son in danger.

## Why we liked it 👍
- Masterful psychological horror that delves into the complexities of the human mind.
- Atmospheric setting and eerie ambiance that builds tension throughout the story.

## Why it might not work for you 👎
- Some readers may find the pacing slow at times.
- Certain scenes can be intense and unsettling for sensitive readers.

## Full spoiler-free review
Stephen King's 'The Shining' is a chilling masterpiece that delves deep into the
darkest corners of the human psyche. The slow descent into madness of Jack
Torrance is both fascinating and terrifying, as the Overlook Hotel's sinister
influence takes hold. King's atmospheric writing creates a palpable sense of
dread, with the isolated, snowbound setting adding to the feeling of
claustrophobia and impending doom. The interactions between the characters are
tense and fraught with underlying tension, keeping readers on edge throughout.
While some may find the pacing slow, the payoff is worth it as the story builds
to a heart-pounding climax. 'The Shining' is a haunting and unforgettable tale
that will linger in your thoughts long after you've turned the final page.
