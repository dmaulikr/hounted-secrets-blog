In 'The Shining,' a struggling writer named Jack Torrance accepts a job as the
winter caretaker of the remote Overlook Hotel, where he moves in with his
family. As the hotel's sinister past begins to influence Jack's behavior, his
son Danny's psychic abilities lead to a terrifying confrontation with the
supernatural forces within the hotel.

## Full spoiler-free review
Stephen King's 'The Shining' is a chilling and immersive tale that delves deep
into the darkness of the human psyche. King expertly crafts an atmosphere of
dread and unease that permeates every page, drawing readers into the haunting
world of the Overlook Hotel. The characters are richly developed, particularly
Jack Torrance, whose descent into madness is both fascinating and terrifying to
witness. The supernatural elements are seamlessly woven into the narrative,
adding an extra layer of horror to the already tense story. While the pacing may
be slow for some, the payoff is well worth it, culminating in a gripping and
unforgettable climax. 'The Shining' is a horror masterpiece that lingers long
after the final page is turned, solidifying its status as a classic in the
genre.
