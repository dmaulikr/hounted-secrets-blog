In 'The Shining', author Stephen King weaves a chilling tale of a family's
descent into madness at the isolated Overlook Hotel during the winter season. As
the supernatural forces within the hotel begin to manipulate the patriarch, Jack
Torrance, his wife Wendy and son Danny must fight for their lives against the
malevolent spirits that haunt the corridors and rooms of the hotel.

## Full spoiler-free review
Stephen King's 'The Shining' is a classic horror novel that delves deep into the
psyche of its characters, offering a terrifying exploration of isolation,
madness, and the supernatural. King's prose is as haunting as the Overlook Hotel
itself, drawing readers into a world where reality blurs with nightmare. The
slow burn of suspense keeps you on edge, while the characters' struggles with
their inner demons add a compelling layer to the horror. While the pacing may
test some readers' patience, the payoff is a chilling climax that will linger in
your thoughts long after you've finished the book. 'The Shining' is a must-read
for horror fans looking for a psychological scare that cuts deep into the soul.
