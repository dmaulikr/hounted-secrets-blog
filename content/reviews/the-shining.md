In 'The Shining,' Jack Torrance takes a job as the winter caretaker of the
isolated Overlook Hotel with his family. As the hotel's dark past begins to
manifest itself, Jack's sanity unravels, putting his wife Wendy and their young
son Danny in grave danger.

## Why we liked it 👍
- Intense and atmospheric setting that creates a palpable sense of dread.
- Complex characters that undergo gripping psychological transformations.

## Why it might not work for you 👎
- Some readers may find certain scenes disturbing and unsettling.
- The pacing can be slow at times, building tension but potentially testing patience.

## Full spoiler-free review
Stephen King's 'The Shining' is a chilling exploration of isolation, madness,
and the supernatural. The Overlook Hotel is a character in itself, with its
malevolent presence seeping into every corner of the story. King masterfully
builds tension through a combination of eerie atmosphere and psychological
horror, making it a gripping read from start to finish. Jack Torrance's descent
into madness is both terrifying and tragic, while Danny's psychic abilities add
an extra layer of intrigue. The dynamic between the characters is fraught with
tension, keeping readers on edge as they navigate the increasingly unsettling
events at the Overlook. 'The Shining' is a classic horror tale that continues to
haunt readers with its lingering sense of unease long after the final page is
turned.
