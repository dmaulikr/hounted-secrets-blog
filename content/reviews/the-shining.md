In 'The Shining,' Jack Torrance takes on a job as the winter caretaker of the
Overlook Hotel, moving in with his wife Wendy and son Danny. As the family
settles in, supernatural occurrences begin to plague them, triggering Jack's
descent into madness. With its eerie setting and psychological horror, the novel
delves into themes of isolation, family dynamics, and the darkness within.

## Why we liked it 👍
- Masterful blend of psychological tension and supernatural elements
- Complex, well-developed characters that add depth to the story

## Why it might not work for you 👎
- Slow pacing in some parts may deter readers looking for quick scares
- Some elements may feel dated to modern audiences

## Full spoiler-free review
Stephen King's 'The Shining' is a masterpiece of horror literature that immerses
readers in a chilling and atmospheric tale of terror. The gradual unraveling of
Jack Torrance's sanity, combined with the psychic abilities of his son Danny,
creates a sense of dread that lingers long after the final page. King's
intricate storytelling weaves a web of suspense, blending the supernatural with
the psychological to create a truly haunting narrative. While the pacing may be
considered slow by some, the character development and tension-building are
impeccable. 'The Shining' is a must-read for horror enthusiasts looking for a
deeply unsettling and thought-provoking experience. Just remember to keep the
lights on while reading.
