In 'The Shining,' Jack Torrance takes on the job of caretaker at the secluded
Overlook Hotel during the winter, bringing his family along. As the hotel's dark
history begins to unravel, Jack's sanity is put to the ultimate test, leading to
a gripping tale of psychological horror and supernatural occurrences.

## Why we liked it 👍
- Intense and atmospheric setting that enhances the sense of isolation and dread.
- Complex and nuanced characters that add depth to the story's unfolding madness.

## Why it might not work for you 👎
- Pacing may feel slow for readers expecting constant action.
- Some themes and scenes may be unsettling for sensitive readers.

## Full spoiler-free review
Stephen King's 'The Shining' is a chilling masterpiece that blends psychological
terror with supernatural elements seamlessly. The Overlook Hotel serves as a
character in itself, looming large and casting a sinister shadow over the
Torrance family. King's intricate exploration of Jack's descent into madness is
both mesmerizing and disturbing, making the reader question what is real and
what is a product of the hotel's malevolent influence. The tension builds
steadily, creating a palpable sense of unease that lingers long after the final
page. While the pacing may be leisurely at times, it allows for a deep dive into
the characters' psyche, making their struggles feel all the more real and
harrowing. 'The Shining' is a must-read for horror enthusiasts looking for a
tale that will haunt them long after they finish the last sentence.
