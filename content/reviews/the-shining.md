In 'The Shining,' Jack Torrance, a struggling writer and recovering alcoholic,
takes a job as the winter caretaker of the isolated Overlook Hotel with his
family. As the supernatural forces within the hotel begin to influence Jack, his
son Danny's psychic abilities reveal dark secrets that threaten their lives.

## Why we liked it 👍
- Masterful atmospheric tension and psychological horror
- Complex characters and intricate storytelling

## Why it might not work for you 👎
- Some readers may find the pacing slow in parts
- Themes of alcoholism and domestic violence may be triggering for some

## Full spoiler-free review
Stephen King's 'The Shining' is a chilling masterpiece that delves deep into the
complexities of the human psyche and the horrors that lurk within. King expertly
crafts a sense of dread and foreboding throughout the novel, building tension
with each turn of the page. The characters are multi-dimensional, flawed, and
utterly compelling, adding layers of depth to the narrative. The exploration of
addiction, isolation, and the supernatural is done with a deft hand, making 'The
Shining' a truly haunting and unforgettable read. While the pacing may be slow
for some readers, the payoff is well worth the journey. King's ability to blend
psychological terror with supernatural elements makes this a classic in the
horror genre that continues to captivate and terrify readers to this day.
