In 'The Shining,' Jack Torrance takes a job as the winter caretaker of the
Overlook Hotel with his wife and son. As the hotel's dark past begins to
influence Jack, his son Danny's psychic abilities reveal terrifying truths about
the place. The isolation and malevolent forces within the hotel drive the family
to their breaking point, resulting in a chilling and suspenseful tale of
psychological horror.

## Full spoiler-free review
Stephen King's 'The Shining' is a winter-cabin masterpiece that delves deep into
the darkness of the human mind. The eerie setting of the Overlook Hotel serves
as a character in itself, with its haunted past and malevolent presence adding
layers of suspense to the story. King's writing is gripping, drawing readers
into the unraveling psyche of Jack Torrance and the supernatural forces at play.
The complex characters, particularly young Danny with his 'shining' ability,
provide a haunting exploration of fear and resilience. While the pacing may be
slow for some, the payoff is a terrifying and unforgettable descent into
madness. 'The Shining' is a must-read for fans of psychological horror,
showcasing King's skill at crafting intricate and unsettling narratives.
