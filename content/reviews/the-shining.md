In 'The Shining,' Jack Torrance accepts a job as the winter caretaker of the
Overlook Hotel in Colorado, bringing his wife Wendy and son Danny along. As the
hotel's sinister past begins to unravel, Danny's psychic abilities shine a light
on the dark forces at play, leading to a chilling and suspenseful tale of
isolation, madness, and supernatural horror.

## Why we liked it 👍
- Intricately crafted characters that delve deep into psychological and emotional turmoil.
- Atmospheric setting that intensifies the sense of dread and unease throughout the story.

## Why it might not work for you 👎
- Pacing may be slow for readers expecting constant action.
- Some scenes may be too intense for those sensitive to graphic violence or disturbing themes.

## Full spoiler-free review
Stephen King's 'The Shining' is a masterclass in psychological horror, blending
supernatural elements with profound character exploration. Jack Torrance's
descent into madness is both gripping and terrifying, making him a compelling
yet deeply unsettling protagonist. King's attention to detail in building the
eerie atmosphere of the Overlook Hotel is unparalleled, immersing readers in a
world where reality blurs with hallucination. The dynamics between the Torrance
family members add layers of complexity to the narrative, heightening the
tension as the story unfolds. While the pacing may be considered slow by some,
the gradual build-up only serves to amplify the sense of impending doom. 'The
Shining' is a classic horror novel that continues to haunt readers with its
intricate plot, well-developed characters, and spine-tingling suspense.
