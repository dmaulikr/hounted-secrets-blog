In 'The Shining', a struggling writer named Jack Torrance takes a job as the
winter caretaker of the isolated Overlook Hotel with his family. As the hotel's
sinister past begins to reveal itself, Jack's sanity unravels, putting his wife
Wendy and psychic son Danny in grave danger.

## Why we liked it 👍
- Masterful tension-building and atmospheric setting.
- Complex characters with psychological depth.

## Why it might not work for you 👎
- Some readers may find the pacing slow.
- Certain supernatural elements may be unsettling for sensitive readers.

## Full spoiler-free review
Stephen King's 'The Shining' is a chilling masterpiece that delves deep into the
psyche of its characters, particularly the tormented Jack Torrance. The slow-
burn tension and eerie atmosphere of the Overlook Hotel create a sense of
creeping dread that keeps readers on edge throughout. King's exploration of
themes like isolation, addiction, and the destructive power of unchecked rage
adds layers of depth to the story. While the pacing may be considered slow by
some, it allows for a gradual escalation of horror that culminates in a
terrifying climax. 'The Shining' is a must-read for horror fans looking for a
psychological thriller that will haunt them long after the final page.
