In 'The Shining,' Jack Torrance takes a job as the winter caretaker at the
Overlook Hotel, where he hopes to find peace and inspiration to work on his
writing. However, the isolation and supernatural forces within the hotel slowly
drive him to madness, putting his family in grave danger.

## Why we liked it 👍
- Intense and atmospheric storytelling that builds tension masterfully.
- Complex and well-developed characters that enhance the psychological horror.

## Why it might not work for you 👎
- Some readers may find certain scenes disturbing or triggering.
- The pacing can be slow at times, focusing more on psychological horror than action.

## Full spoiler-free review
Stephen King's 'The Shining' is a chilling masterpiece that delves deep into the
psyche of its characters, particularly Jack Torrance, whose descent into madness
is both terrifying and tragic. King's writing is immersive, drawing readers into
the eerie and claustrophobic atmosphere of the Overlook Hotel. The supernatural
elements are woven seamlessly into the narrative, adding an extra layer of dread
to the already unsettling story. While the pacing may be slower than some horror
novels, the gradual build-up of tension only serves to heighten the sense of
impending doom. Overall, 'The Shining' is a must-read for fans of psychological
horror, offering a haunting exploration of isolation, madness, and the darkness
that lurks within us all.
