In 'The Shining,' Stephen King tells the chilling story of the Torrance family
as they care for a remote hotel during the winter. As the isolation and
supernatural forces within the hotel begin to take their toll, Jack Torrance's
descent into madness threatens his wife and son, Danny, who possesses psychic
abilities known as 'the shining.' The novel explores themes of addiction, family
dynamics, and the haunting past that manifests in the present.

## Why we liked it 👍
- Masterful atmospheric setting that intensifies the sense of dread.
- Complex characters that add layers of psychological horror.

## Why it might not work for you 👎
- Pacing may feel slow for readers seeking immediate scares.
- Some scenes may be too intense for sensitive readers.

## Full spoiler-free review
Stephen King's 'The Shining' remains a timeless classic in the horror genre. The
eerie atmosphere of the remote Overlook Hotel serves as a character in itself,
adding a palpable sense of foreboding to the narrative. King's portrayal of the
gradual unraveling of Jack Torrance's sanity is a masterclass in psychological
horror, exploring the thin line between the supernatural and the darkness
within. The dynamic between Jack, Wendy, and young Danny is fraught with tension
and fear, keeping readers on edge throughout. While the pacing may be a bit slow
for some, the payoff is well worth it, leading to a climax that is both
terrifying and thought-provoking. 'The Shining' is a must-read for fans of
horror literature, showcasing King's unparalleled ability to tap into our
deepest fears.
