In 'Ghost Light', a haunted theater becomes the stage for chilling encounters
with restless spirits and mysterious occurrences. As the cast of a new
production delves into the history of the theater, they uncover dark secrets and
a curse that threatens to consume them all.

## Why we liked it 👍
- Eerie atmosphere that keeps readers on edge
- Intriguing blend of supernatural elements with the world of theater

## Why it might not work for you 👎
- Pacing may feel slow at times
- Some plot twists may be predictable for seasoned horror readers

## Full spoiler-free review
 'Ghost Light' is a captivating blend of ghostly encounters and theatrical
intrigue. The author expertly weaves a sense of foreboding throughout the story,
creating an atmosphere that is both eerie and immersive. The exploration of the
theater's history and the curse that haunts it adds depth to the narrative,
keeping readers guessing until the very end. While the pacing may feel slow in
some parts, the tension steadily builds, leading to a chilling climax that is
sure to leave readers with a lingering sense of unease. Fans of both horror and
the performing arts will find 'Ghost Light' to be a compelling read that offers
a unique twist on the haunted house trope. Overall, 'Ghost Light' is a haunting
tale that will keep you turning pages late into the night.
