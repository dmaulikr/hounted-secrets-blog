In 'Ghost Light', a haunted theater becomes the backdrop for a series of
chilling events as a troupe of actors find themselves at the mercy of vengeful
spirits. The discovery of an ancient cursed script sets off a chain of
terrifying occurrences that threaten to consume all who dare to set foot on the
stage.

## Why we liked it 👍
- Atmospheric setting that brings the eerie world of the theater to life.
- Well-developed characters that draw readers into their escalating nightmare.

## Why it might not work for you 👎
- Pacing occasionally falters, with some sections feeling drawn out.
- Certain plot twists may be predictable for seasoned horror readers.

## Full spoiler-free review
 'Ghost Light' immerses readers in a spine-tingling tale that blends the
supernatural with the world of the performing arts. The author skillfully weaves
together elements of ghostly lore and theatrical superstitions to create a
haunting atmosphere that lingers long after the final curtain falls. While the
pacing may lag at times, the characters' descent into madness and the escalating
sense of dread keep the reader engaged. The cursed script adds a unique twist to
the classic haunted house trope, offering a fresh take on familiar themes. Fans
of ghost stories and eerie settings will find much to enjoy in 'Ghost Light', as
it delivers a satisfying blend of suspense and supernatural horror that will
leave readers looking over their shoulders long after they finish the final
page.
