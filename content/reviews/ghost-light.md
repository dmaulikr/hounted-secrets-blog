In 'Ghost Light', a haunted theater becomes the backdrop for a series of
chilling events as stage-door spirits and cursed scripts come to life. The
play's characters must confront their darkest fears as they navigate a world
where reality and the supernatural collide.

## Why we liked it 👍
- Atmospheric setting that immerses readers in the eerie world of the theater
- Well-developed characters with complex relationships and motivations

## Why it might not work for you 👎
- Pacing can be slow at times, dragging out the suspense
- Some plot twists may feel predictable to seasoned horror readers

## Full spoiler-free review
 'Ghost Light' is a haunting tale that will send shivers down your spine. The
author expertly weaves together elements of the supernatural with the rich
history of the theater, creating a chilling atmosphere that lingers long after
the final curtain falls. The characters are compelling and flawed, each
grappling with their own demons as they navigate the increasingly sinister
events unfolding around them. While the pacing may lag in some parts, the slow
build of tension only serves to heighten the sense of dread. The twists and
turns in the plot are well-executed, though some seasoned horror readers may see
them coming. Overall, 'Ghost Light' is a captivating read that will appeal to
fans of atmospheric horror and ghostly mysteries.
