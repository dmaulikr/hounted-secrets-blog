In 'Ghost Light,' a haunted theater becomes the stage for a series of chilling
events as a cursed script unleashes vengeful spirits. As the cast and crew
struggle to survive, they must confront the dark secrets that have long lingered
within the theater's walls.

## Why we liked it 👍
- Atmospheric setting that enhances the sense of dread and unease.
- Intriguing exploration of the intersection between superstition and reality.

## Why it might not work for you 👎
- Pacing occasionally falters, leading to moments of inconsistency.
- Some character development feels underdeveloped, detracting from emotional investment.

## Full spoiler-free review
 'Ghost Light' delivers a spine-tingling tale set in the eerie confines of a
haunted theater. The author skillfully weaves together elements of paranormal
activity, mystery, and psychological horror to create a gripping narrative. The
atmospheric setting plays a central role in building tension, with the old
theater becoming a character in its own right. While the pacing may stumble at
times, the novel's overall sense of foreboding and suspense keeps readers
engaged. The exploration of superstition and its impact on reality adds a
thought-provoking layer to the story. Though some character development feels
lacking, the diverse cast of characters each brings a unique perspective to the
unfolding terror. 'Ghost Light' is a haunting read that will leave you looking
over your shoulder long after you've turned the final page.
