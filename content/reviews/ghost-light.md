In 'Ghost Light', a haunted theater becomes the backdrop for a series of eerie
occurrences as a young actress uncovers dark secrets hidden within the stage.
With mysterious apparitions and a cursed script threatening her sanity, she must
confront the malevolent forces at play before it's too late.

## Why we liked it 👍
- Atmospheric setting that enhances the chilling atmosphere of the story.
- Well-developed characters that draw readers into the unfolding mystery.

## Why it might not work for you 👎
- Pacing can be slow at times, especially in the middle of the book.
- Some plot points may feel predictable to seasoned horror readers.

## Full spoiler-free review
 'Ghost Light' is a spine-tingling tale that perfectly captures the eerie allure
of a haunted theater. The author skillfully weaves together elements of
supernatural horror and psychological suspense to create a gripping narrative
that keeps readers on the edge of their seats. The atmospheric setting of the
theater is vividly portrayed, adding a sense of dread and foreboding to the
story. While the pacing may lag in some sections, the well-developed characters
and the gradual unraveling of the mystery make up for it. Fans of ghost stories
and haunted settings will find plenty to enjoy in 'Ghost Light'. Overall, this
is a hauntingly atmospheric read that delivers plenty of chills and thrills for
those brave enough to step into its sinister world.
