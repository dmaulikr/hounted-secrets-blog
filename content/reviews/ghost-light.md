In 'Ghost Light,' a haunted theater becomes the backdrop for chilling
occurrences as a group of actors rehearse a cursed play. Mysterious deaths,
ghostly apparitions, and a malevolent presence lurking in the shadows create a
truly spine-tingling atmosphere that will keep readers on edge until the final
curtain call.

## Why we liked it 👍
- Atmospheric setting that enhances the eerie ambiance of the story
- Well-developed characters that add depth to the supernatural elements

## Why it might not work for you 👎
- Pacing can be slow at times, particularly in the middle of the book
- Some plot points may feel predictable to seasoned horror readers

## Full spoiler-free review
 'Ghost Light' is a haunting tale that skillfully weaves together elements of
the supernatural with the world of theater. The author excels in creating a
sense of foreboding through vivid descriptions of the dilapidated theater and
its ghostly inhabitants. The characters are compelling and flawed, making their
interactions with the supernatural entities all the more chilling. While the
pacing may lag in parts, the slow build only serves to heighten the suspense as
the story hurtles towards a shocking conclusion. Fans of ghost stories and
atmospheric horror will find 'Ghost Light' to be a captivating read that lingers
long after the final page is turned.
