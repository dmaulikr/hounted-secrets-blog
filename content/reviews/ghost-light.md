In 'Ghost Light', a renowned theater is plagued by mysterious occurrences linked
to a cursed script and vengeful spirits haunting the stage. As the cast and crew
delve deeper into the play's dark history, they uncover chilling secrets that
blur the line between reality and the supernatural.

## Why we liked it 👍
- Atmospheric setting that enhances the eerie ambiance of the story
- Intriguing exploration of the intersection between art and the paranormal

## Why it might not work for you 👎
- Some plot points may feel predictable to seasoned horror readers
- Character development could be more robust

## Full spoiler-free review
 'Ghost Light' is a haunting tale that cleverly combines the world of theater
with supernatural elements. The author excels at creating a palpable sense of
dread through vivid descriptions of the eerie setting and the malevolent spirits
that lurk within. The exploration of the cursed script and its connection to the
ghostly presence adds layers of mystery and intrigue to the narrative. While the
plot may follow some familiar horror tropes, the unique backdrop of a theater
infuses the story with a fresh perspective. However, the character development
could have been more robust to fully engage readers in the emotional stakes of
the haunting. Overall, 'Ghost Light' is a spine-tingling read that will appeal
to fans of atmospheric horror and ghostly mysteries.
