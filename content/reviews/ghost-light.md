In 'Ghost Light', a theater production is plagued by mysterious occurrences,
from ghostly apparitions to unexplained accidents. As the cast and crew delve
deeper into the history of the theater, they uncover a dark secret that
threatens to consume them all.

## Why we liked it 👍
- Atmospheric setting that immerses readers in the eerie world of the theater
- Well-developed characters with complex relationships and motivations

## Why it might not work for you 👎
- Pacing may lag at times, especially in the middle of the book
- Some plot twists may feel predictable to seasoned horror readers

## Full spoiler-free review
 'Ghost Light' is a chilling tale that blends elements of supernatural horror
with the dramatic backdrop of a theater production. The author expertly weaves
together themes of ambition, betrayal, and the supernatural, creating a story
that keeps readers on the edge of their seats. The atmospheric setting of the
old theater lends an air of creepiness to the narrative, making it easy to
imagine the ghostly presences lurking in the shadows. While the pacing may slow
down in parts, the well-developed characters and their intertwined relationships
add depth to the story. Despite some predictable plot twists, 'Ghost Light'
offers a satisfying mix of suspense and supernatural elements that will leave
readers eagerly turning pages to uncover the dark secrets hidden within the
theater's walls.
