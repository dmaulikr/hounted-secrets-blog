In 'Ghost Light', the theater world is plagued by supernatural occurrences as a
haunted production becomes a playground for vengeful spirits. The play's cast
and crew must confront their darkest secrets and the malevolent force lurking
within the theater's shadows.

## Why we liked it 👍
- Atmospheric setting that captures the eerie charm of a haunted theater
- Well-developed characters with intriguing backstories

## Why it might not work for you 👎
- Pacing can be slow at times, especially in the buildup to major scares
- Some plot elements may feel predictable to seasoned horror readers

## Full spoiler-free review
 'Ghost Light' weaves a haunting tale of the supernatural within the world of
theater, blending the art of storytelling with spine-chilling horror. The
atmospheric setting of the haunted theater creates a sense of dread that lingers
throughout the narrative, enhanced by the well-developed characters who each
carry their own secrets and fears. While the pacing may drag at points, the
payoff in terms of scares and tension is worth the wait. The author excels in
building a sense of foreboding, leading readers through a twisted plot filled
with unexpected twists and chilling revelations. 'Ghost Light' is a captivating
read for those who enjoy a mix of psychological horror and ghostly encounters,
offering a unique take on the classic haunted house trope that will leave you
questioning what lurks behind the curtain.
