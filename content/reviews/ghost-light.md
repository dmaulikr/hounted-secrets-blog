In 'Ghost Light,' a historic theater becomes the center of supernatural
occurrences as actors and crew members are haunted by mysterious apparitions and
strange events. The discovery of a cursed script sets off a chain of terrifying
events that threaten to consume everyone involved in the production. As the line
between reality and the paranormal blurs, the characters must confront their
deepest fears to unravel the dark secrets hidden within the theater's walls.

## Why we liked it 👍
- Atmospheric setting that immerses readers in the eerie world of the theater
- Well-developed characters with complex relationships and motivations

## Why it might not work for you 👎
- Pacing can be slow at times, especially in the build-up to major reveals
- Some plot points may feel predictable to seasoned horror readers

## Full spoiler-free review
 'Ghost Light' is a chilling tale that effectively blends the world of theater
with supernatural horror. The author skillfully creates a sense of unease and
tension throughout the story, drawing readers into a sinister world where the
boundaries between the living and the dead are blurred. The atmospheric setting
of the historic theater adds a layer of authenticity to the haunting events that
unfold, making the story all the more immersive. While the pacing may lag at
points, the well-developed characters and their intricate relationships keep the
narrative engaging. The gradual unraveling of the cursed script's secrets builds
suspense and sets the stage for a gripping climax. Fans of ghostly mysteries and
haunted settings will find 'Ghost Light' to be a captivating read that delivers
on its promise of spine-tingling scares.
