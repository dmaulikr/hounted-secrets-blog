In 'Ghost Light,' a haunted theater becomes the setting for a series of eerie
events as the spirits of past performers seek revenge on those who have wronged
them. As a cursed script resurfaces, the line between fiction and reality blurs,
leading to a chilling tale of betrayal and supernatural forces at play.

## Why we liked it 👍
- Atmospheric setting that immerses readers in the eerie world of the theater
- Well-developed characters with complex motivations and secrets

## Why it might not work for you 👎
- Pacing may feel slow at times, particularly in the buildup to the climax
- Some plot elements may feel predictable to seasoned horror readers

## Full spoiler-free review
 'Ghost Light' is a haunting read that skillfully weaves together elements of
supernatural horror and psychological suspense. The author's vivid descriptions
bring the haunted theater to life, making it feel like a character in its own
right. The diverse cast of characters adds depth to the story, each with their
own dark secrets and hidden agendas that keep the reader guessing. While the
pacing may be leisurely at times, the tension steadily builds, culminating in a
chilling and satisfying conclusion. Fans of atmospheric horror will appreciate
the immersive setting and the sinister presence of the vengeful spirits lurking
in the shadows. 'Ghost Light' is a captivating and spooky tale that will keep
readers on edge until the final curtain falls.
