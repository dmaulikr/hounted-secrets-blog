In 'Ghost Light,' a theater troupe discovers dark secrets behind a supposedly
haunted stage. As the actors delve into the eerie history of the theater, they
uncover a malevolent force that threatens to consume them all. With ghostly
apparitions and cursed scripts, the line between performance and reality blurs,
leading to a chilling climax that will leave readers questioning what is truly
lurking in the shadows of the stage.

## Why we liked it 👍
- Atmospheric setting that immerses readers in the world of the theater
- Intriguing blend of supernatural elements and theatrical intrigue

## Why it might not work for you 👎
- Pacing may be slow for readers looking for quick scares
- Some characters lack depth and development

## Full spoiler-free review
 'Ghost Light' weaves a haunting tale of the supernatural within the confines of
a theater, blending elements of ghostly encounters with the drama of the stage.
The author skillfully creates a sense of foreboding throughout the narrative,
building tension as the characters uncover the dark secrets of the theater's
past. While the pacing may be leisurely at times, the atmospheric setting and
well-crafted suspense keep readers engaged. However, some characters feel
underdeveloped, lacking the depth needed to fully invest in their fates.
Overall, 'Ghost Light' is a chilling read that will appeal to fans of both
horror and theater, offering a unique blend of spectral encounters and backstage
drama that culminates in a spine-tingling finale.
