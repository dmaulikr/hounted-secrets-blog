In 'Ghost Light', a haunted theater becomes the backdrop for a series of
unexplained events as a troupe of actors rehearse a cursed play. Strange
occurrences, ghostly apparitions, and dark secrets from the theater's past come
to light, threatening the lives of those involved in the production.

## Why we liked it 👍
- Atmospheric setting that creates a chilling and eerie ambiance
- Well-developed characters with intriguing backstories

## Why it might not work for you 👎
- Pacing may be slow for readers looking for rapid scares
- Some plot elements may feel predictable to seasoned horror fans

## Full spoiler-free review
 'Ghost Light' immerses readers in a haunting tale set within the confines of a
theater steeped in history and mystery. The author skillfully builds tension
through a combination of atmospheric descriptions and eerie encounters, keeping
the reader on edge throughout. The characters are compelling and well-drawn,
each harboring their own secrets that add layers to the unfolding drama. While
the pace may be leisurely at times, it allows for a deeper exploration of the
characters' motivations and the sinister forces at play. Fans of supernatural
horror will appreciate the ghostly manifestations and the sense of creeping
dread that permeates the narrative. Overall, 'Ghost Light' is a solid entry in
the genre, offering a blend of psychological unease and spectral hauntings that
will leave readers looking over their shoulders long after the final curtain
falls.
